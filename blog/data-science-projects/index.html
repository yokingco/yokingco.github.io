STAC53H3: Applied Data Collection

Project Title: The Association Between Systolic Blood Pressure and Various Demographic, Lifestyle, and Physiological Characteristics of the American Public: An Applied Statistical Analysis

Runners on a track. Photo by Steven Lelham.

Read the report here.

This was a final project for my Applied Data Collection course. The objective of this project was to analyze the relationship between systolic blood pressure (SBP) and smoking exposure as well as build a linear regression model to predict average SBP from multiple exposure variables (e.g. age, education, marital status, etc.). The representative sample used was generated based on two different sampling methods (simple random sampling and stratified random sampling) from the American National Health and Nutrition Examination survey data (NHANES dataset).
